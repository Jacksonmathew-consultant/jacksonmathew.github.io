Problem & Goal

A major medical device manufacturer was hampered by an outdated system for tracking device usage. The process was manual, error-prone, and slow, creating compliance risks and operational bottlenecks. The primary goal was to develop a modern, centralized enterprise application to automate tracking, improve reporting accuracy, and predict maintenance needs to prevent downtime.

My Role & Responsibilities

As the Project Manager and Business Analyst, I led the project from concept to launch. My responsibilities included: gathering and documenting requirements from stakeholders (technicians, compliance officers), creating user stories, managing the product backlog in Jira, facilitating all Agile/Scrum ceremonies (sprints, stand-ups, retrospectives), and serving as the primary liaison between the 15-person development team and executive leadership.

Forklift Fleet Optimization Strategy

Using data analysis to drive significant cost savings in heavy equipment management.

Project Snapshot

Company: Lamprell Energy

My Role: Business Optimization Analyst

Tools & Frameworks

Excel (Pareto, Cost Analysis)

Root Cause Analysis (RCA)

Asset Management

Business Problem

The company's forklift fleet of 35 units was a significant operational expense, but there was no data-driven strategy for its management. This resulted in excessive maintenance costs, poor utilization, and missed opportunities for cost savings.

Challenges & My Approach

The primary challenge was the poor quality and fragmentation of historical data. Maintenance logs were inconsistent, and meter readings were often recorded inaccurately. I dedicated the initial phase to data cleaning and validation, collaborating with the maintenance team to reconcile discrepancies. I then used Pareto analysis in Excel to identify that just a few aging machines were responsible for a disproportionate amount of costs, which became the cornerstone of my business case.

Outcomes & Business Impact

  • Identified $172K+ in Annual Savings: The data-driven recommendation to scrap 4 specific high-maintenance forklifts and optimize the hire/own strategy was projected to save over $14K per month.
  • Revealed 44% Underutilization: The analysis brought to light the fleet's poor overall efficiency, sparking a wider conversation about resource scheduling and planning.
  • Created a Data-Driven Framework: This project established a new, analytical framework for all future asset management decisions, moving the company from reactive maintenance to proactive, cost-conscious fleet strategy.

Key Lessons Learned

Never underestimate the power of foundational data quality. The success of any analysis hinges on reliable data, and investing time in cleaning and validating upfront is crucial for generating credible, actionable insights that leadership can trust.

Welder Performance & Quality Improvement

A Lean Six Sigma approach to reducing production delays and improving weld quality.

Project Snapshot

Company: Lamprell Energy

My Role: Project Leader (LSS Green Belt)

Tools & Frameworks

Lean Six Sigma (DMAIC)

Root Cause Analysis (Fishbone, 5 Whys)

Statistical Analysis

Business Problem

A critical energy project was plagued by production delays caused by a bottleneck in the welding inspection process. The time from weld completion to the final NDT was unacceptably long, and a high NDT rejection rate of 18.15% was forcing costly rework.

Challenges & My Approach

The challenge was cultural. The production floor was resistant to what they perceived as "ivory tower" analysis. To build trust, I spent significant time on the floor, observing workflows and interviewing welders and inspectors directly. This hands-on approach, combined with data from a Fishbone diagram workshop, allowed me to pinpoint that pre-inspection grinding—a low-skill task—was consuming 16% of highly-skilled welders' time. This tangible, easily understood finding quickly gained me credibility with the team.

Outcomes & Business Impact

  • Identified $20,000/Month in Savings: By quantifying the time welders spent on grinding, I built a successful business case to create a dedicated, lower-cost grinding team, delivering an immediate ROI.
  • Pinpointed Root Causes of Defects: The analysis provided clear evidence linking high defect rates to specific joint types and inadequate training, allowing for targeted improvement actions.
  • Data-Driven HR Strategy: The findings provided HR with a data-driven basis for refining their welder selection and training programs.

Key Lessons Learned

Quantitative data is powerful, but qualitative, on-the-ground insights are essential for context and buy-in. To drive real change in an operational environment, you must build relationships and demonstrate that your analysis is rooted in the reality of the work being done.

Scrap Metal Value Recovery Initiative

Applying Lean principles to turn operational waste into a significant revenue stream.

Project Snapshot

Company: Lamprell Energy

My Role: Business Optimization Analyst

Tools & Frameworks

Lean (Value Stream Mapping)

ROI Analysis

Process Improvement

Business Problem

The company was selling scrap copper cable at a blended, low rate, approximately 50% below the market price for pure copper. This represented a significant and untapped revenue opportunity, as the high-value copper was not being segregated from its low-value insulation.

Challenges & My Approach

The challenge was not analytical, but operational. I had to coordinate with three different departments—Operations, Supply Chain, and HSES—each with their own priorities, to implement a new physical process. I created a simple, one-page process map and a clear RACI chart to define roles and responsibilities. By clearly articulating the financial benefit and making the new process easy to adopt, I was able to secure the cross-functional collaboration needed for a smooth implementation.

Outcomes & Business Impact

  • Immediate 665% ROI: The very first batch of segregated copper generated a net profit of $12,521 on an initial investment of just $1,882.
  • Generated $14.4K in Additional Revenue: The segregated copper from the first batch sold for a 39% higher price than it would have as mixed scrap.
  • Created a Sustainable Revenue Stream: The project established a new, ongoing process projected to generate an additional $30,000+ annually from what was previously considered waste.

Key Lessons Learned

A brilliant idea is worthless without execution. For process improvement projects, clear communication, simple documentation (like a one-page process map), and defined ownership are just as important as the financial analysis itself.

WM Decarbonization Strategy

A strategic proposal for Waste Management Inc.'s transition to a sustainable future.

Project Snapshot

Category: Corporate Strategy (Competition)

My Role: Strategic Analyst

Timeline: A4S International Case Competition 2024

Frameworks & Skills

Financial Modeling

Corporate Strategy

Climate Transition Planning

Stakeholder Impact Analysis

Executive Summary

For the A4S (Accounting for Sustainability) competition, my team and I developed a strategic proposal for Waste Management, Inc. (WM), North America's leading environmental services provider. Our challenge was to create a financially viable roadmap for WM to address its carbon-intensive operations and lead the climate transition within its industry. Our solution focused on a two-pronged approach: tackling landfill emissions and decarbonizing their vehicle fleet.

The Problem: A Two-Fold Carbon Challenge

WM faces significant environmental challenges. First, its landfills are major sources of methane, a greenhouse gas over 80 times more potent than CO2. Second, its vast fleet of diesel-powered collection vehicles contributes significantly to carbon emissions. Our task was to devise solutions that not only mitigate this impact but also create long-term value for the business.

My Role & Responsibilities

As a strategic analyst on the team, my responsibilities included:

  • Analyzing WM's operational and financial data to identify key areas for carbon reduction.
  • Researching and evaluating the feasibility of technologies like landfill gas-to-energy and electric vehicle fleets.
  • Building the business case for our proposed solutions, including initial cost, ROI, and long-term financial implications.
  • Framing our recommendations to align with global sustainability goals and enhance WM's brand reputation.

The Solution: Gas Capture & Fleet Electrification

Our strategy was centered on two high-impact initiatives:

  1. Landfill Gas (LFG) Capture & Utilization: Implementing advanced systems to capture methane emissions from landfills and convert this gas into renewable natural gas (RNG), a clean-burning fuel. This turns a major liability into a revenue-generating asset.
  2. Fleet Electrification: A phased transition from diesel-powered trucks to a fully electric fleet. This directly reduces carbon emissions, lowers fuel and maintenance costs, and improves urban air quality.

Business Impact & KPIs

Our financial and environmental models projected significant returns on these investments:

  • Enhanced Profitability: Reduce operational costs by 20% and generate up to 10% in new revenue from waste-to-energy sales, while avoiding up to $1 million in potential violation fines.
  • Environmental Impact: Cut greenhouse gas emissions by 30% and conserve 20% of natural resources.
  • Improved Customer Satisfaction: Boost customer loyalty and brand reputation, leading to a projected 15% increase in satisfaction.

Alignment with Global Goals

Our proposed solutions directly align with key UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), including SDG 13 (Climate Action), SDG 7 (Affordable and Clean Energy), and SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities), positioning WM as a leader in the circular economy.

Key Learnings

This project was a fantastic exercise in integrating sustainability into core business strategy. The key takeaway was that environmental initiatives don't have to be cost centers. With the right technology and a solid business case, decarbonization can be a powerful driver of profitability, efficiency, and long-term competitive advantage.

Sustainabite: Surplus Food Redistribution

A data-driven product strategy to combat food waste and insecurity in Leeds.

Project Snapshot

Executive Summary

Sustainabite is a product concept for a B2B2C tech platform designed to create a hyper-local, efficient marketplace for surplus food. The platform connects supermarkets with excess inventory to community organizations like university stores and charities, tackling the concurrent problems of food waste and food insecurity. By leveraging AI for logistics and blockchain for transparency, Sustainabite aims to create a commercially viable solution that delivers significant social and environmental impact, starting with a pilot in Leeds.

The Problem Space: A Market Failure in Food Logistics

Our research uncovered a significant market failure in Leeds: Supermarkets waste an estimated £100 million in food annually, while 22% of local households experience food insecurity. This includes over 40,000 university students facing financial hardship. The core problem is a lack of an efficient, trusted, and real-time system to redirect perfectly edible surplus food from suppliers to those in need before it expires.

My Role & Responsibilities

As the product lead, I co-developed the end-to-end strategy, focusing on:

  • Market Analysis: Quantifying the problem and sizing the opportunity.
  • Strategic Planning: Conducting a full SWOT analysis to define our strategic position and potential risks.
  • Product Definition: Defining the core features, user flows, and technology stack.
  • Business Case: Building a comprehensive business model, including a 4-year financial forecast and go-to-market plan.

Strategic Analysis: Finding Our Niche

A SWOT analysis was critical in shaping our strategy. While we identified weaknesses like high initial costs and dependence on supermarkets, we found a compelling opportunity. Existing solutions lacked the technological integration for efficient, city-wide logistics. Our key strength and differentiator became the integration of AI and blockchain to provide a transparent, reliable, and scalable B2B solution that enhances Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) for our supermarket partners.

The Solution: An AI-Powered B2B2C Logistics Platform

Sustainabite is envisioned as a seamless digital platform connecting surplus food supply with community demand. The core technology stack would leverage:

  • AI-Powered Logistics: To optimize collection routes for City Council partners, minimizing fuel costs and time.
  • Predictive Analytics: To forecast surplus generation and match it with real-time demand from university stores and charities.
  • Blockchain for Transparency: To create an immutable record of the food's journey, ensuring accountability and building trust with all stakeholders.
  • Mobile-First Interface: For supermarkets to easily log surplus and for students and charities to view available, discounted food.

Business Model & Go-To-Market Strategy

We designed a multi-stream revenue model to ensure sustainability:

  1. B2B Subscription: Supermarkets pay a fee for waste reduction services and CSR reporting.
  2. Public Sector Grants: Partnering with Leeds City Council to secure funding for sustainability initiatives.
  3. Transaction Fees: A small commission from university stores on goods sold at a reduced price.

Our go-to-market is a phased 12-month plan, starting with a lean pilot of 5 supermarkets to validate the model before scaling city-wide. This de-risks the investment and allows for iterative product improvements based on real-world data.

Measuring Success: KPIs & Projected Impact

Our primary success metric is **Value of food redistributed annually (£)**. Based on our financial model, we project profitability by Year 2. By Year 4, the platform is forecast to:

  • Partner with 30 supermarkets.
  • Generate nearly £100,000 in annual net profit.
  • Redistribute food supporting over 2,000 students and 1,500 elderly individuals.
  • Prevent over 225,000 kg of CO2 emissions per year.

Alignment with Global Goals

The project was explicitly designed to align with three UN Sustainable Development Goals: SDG 2 (Zero Hunger), SDG 10 (Reduced Inequalities), and SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities), making it a compelling proposition for public-private partnerships.

Key Learnings

This project was a deep dive into designing a multi-sided platform. The biggest takeaway was the need for a robust strategy to solve the "chicken and egg" problem. Our phased approach, focusing first on securing a critical mass of supermarket suppliers, was designed specifically to create the necessary value to attract and retain demand-side users (students and charities). The detailed financial modeling also proved that a business can "do good" and "do well" simultaneously.

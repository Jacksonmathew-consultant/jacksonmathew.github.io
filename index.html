Medical Device Management App

Driving efficiency and accuracy in healthcare with AI-powered enterprise solutions.

Project Snapshot

Company: Leading IT Solutions Provider

My Role: Project Manager, Business Analyst

Timeline: 5 Months

Tools & Frameworks

Jira, Confluence

Power BI

Agile, Scrum

Predictive Analytics

Problem & Goal

A major medical device manufacturer was hampered by an outdated system for tracking device usage. The process was manual, error-prone, and slow, creating compliance risks and operational bottlenecks. The primary goal was to develop a modern, centralized enterprise application to automate tracking, improve reporting accuracy, and predict maintenance needs to prevent downtime.

My Role & Responsibilities

As the Project Manager and Business Analyst, I led the project from concept to launch. My responsibilities included: gathering and documenting requirements from stakeholders (technicians, compliance officers), creating user stories, managing the product backlog in Jira, facilitating all Agile/Scrum ceremonies (sprints, stand-ups, retrospectives), and serving as the primary liaison between the 15-person development team and executive leadership.

Challenges & My Approach

One of the primary challenges was navigating conflicting priorities between the compliance department, which required robust audit trails, and the field technicians, who needed a fast, streamlined mobile interface. To resolve this, I facilitated a series of workshops using journey mapping to find common ground. This collaborative process led to a prioritized backlog in Jira that balanced compliance needs with user experience, securing buy-in from both groups. I also championed the allocation of 15% of each sprint to addressing technical debt from the legacy system, a decision I justified to leadership with a clear ROI based on projected long-term development velocity.

Outcomes & User Impact

The new application was a transformative success, delivering significant, measurable value:

  • 30% Boost in Reporting Accuracy: AI-driven analytics and reduced manual entry eliminated common errors, ensuring data integrity.
  • 20% Faster Project Timelines: Clear documentation and agile execution accelerated development and deployment.
  • 15% Increase in Team Efficiency: Automated workflows and KPI monitoring freed up valuable technician and administrative time.
  • 40% Reduction in User-Reported Errors: The intuitive UI design drastically improved the daily experience for field technicians.

Visuals & Deliverables

Key deliverables included detailed process flow diagrams, interactive wireframes, and the comprehensive product roadmap. The Power BI dashboard provided executives with real-time insights into device performance and maintenance forecasts.

Key Lessons Learned

This project reinforced the value of proactive stakeholder management. By bringing opposing groups into the same room early, we converted potential roadblocks into collaborative design decisions. It also taught me the importance of advocating for technical health as a core product feature, not just a 'nice-to-have'.

IoT-Based Automated Climate Monitoring

Eliminating manual checks and mitigating compliance risks with a real-time sensor network.

Project Snapshot

Company: Multinational Energy Firm

My Role: Business Optimization Analyst

Tools & Frameworks

Process Automation

IoT (iMonnit Sensors)

Cost-Benefit Analysis

Change Management

Business Problem

The company relied on a manual process for monitoring temperature and humidity in 10 climate-controlled storage areas. This process was labor-intensive (consuming 2.5 hours daily), prone to gaps in data, and lacked an immediate alert system for climate breaches, posing a significant risk to high-value, sensitive materials.

Challenges & My Approach

The main challenge was securing buy-in from a risk-averse operations team accustomed to manual processes. They were skeptical about the reliability of new technology. To overcome this, I proposed a small-scale, low-cost proof-of-concept (POC) with just three sensors. I managed the POC, presenting weekly reliability reports to stakeholders. The flawless performance of the POC built the confidence needed for a full-scale rollout.

Outcomes & Business Impact

  • 100% Elimination of Manual Checks: The automated system completely replaced the manual logging process, freeing up 2.5 hours of labor daily for more value-added tasks.
  • Real-Time Risk Mitigation: The instant alert system allowed for immediate corrective action, preventing potential spoilage of sensitive materials and ensuring 100% compliance with client and internal quality standards.
  • Enhanced Data Granularity: The system provided a continuous stream of climate data, offering deeper insights into environmental fluctuations.

Key Lessons Learned

A phased approach is critical for introducing new technology into traditional environments. A successful, data-driven POC is the most powerful tool for overcoming skepticism and demonstrating value, making stakeholder management and change adoption significantly smoother.

Forklift Fleet Optimization Strategy

Using data analysis to drive significant cost savings in heavy equipment management.

Project Snapshot

Company: Multinational Energy Firm

My Role: Business Optimization Analyst

Tools & Frameworks

Excel (Pareto, Cost Analysis)

Root Cause Analysis (RCA)

Asset Management

Business Problem

The company's forklift fleet of 35 units was a significant operational expense, but there was no data-driven strategy for its management. This resulted in excessive maintenance costs, poor utilization, and missed opportunities for cost savings.

Challenges & My Approach

The primary challenge was the poor quality and fragmentation of historical data. Maintenance logs were inconsistent, and meter readings were often recorded inaccurately. I dedicated the initial phase to data cleaning and validation, collaborating with the maintenance team to reconcile discrepancies. I then used Pareto analysis in Excel to identify that just a few aging machines were responsible for a disproportionate amount of costs, which became the cornerstone of my business case.

Outcomes & Business Impact

  • Identified $172K+ in Annual Savings: The data-driven recommendation to scrap 4 specific high-maintenance forklifts and optimize the hire/own strategy was projected to save over $14K per month.
  • Revealed 44% Underutilization: The analysis brought to light the fleet's poor overall efficiency, sparking a wider conversation about resource scheduling and planning.
  • Created a Data-Driven Framework: This project established a new, analytical framework for all future asset management decisions, moving the company from reactive maintenance to proactive, cost-conscious fleet strategy.

Key Lessons Learned

Never underestimate the power of foundational data quality. The success of any analysis hinges on reliable data, and investing time in cleaning and validating upfront is crucial for generating credible, actionable insights that leadership can trust.

Welder Performance & Quality Improvement

A Lean Six Sigma approach to reducing production delays and improving weld quality.

Project Snapshot

Company: Multinational Energy Firm

My Role: Project Leader (LSS Green Belt)

Tools & Frameworks

Lean Six Sigma (DMAIC)

Root Cause Analysis (Fishbone, 5 Whys)

Statistical Analysis

Business Problem

A critical energy project was plagued by production delays caused by a bottleneck in the welding inspection process. The time from weld completion to the final NDT was unacceptably long, and a high NDT rejection rate of 18.15% was forcing costly rework.

Challenges & My Approach

The challenge was cultural. The production floor was resistant to what they perceived as "ivory tower" analysis. To build trust, I spent significant time on the floor, observing workflows and interviewing welders and inspectors directly. This hands-on approach, combined with data from a Fishbone diagram workshop, allowed me to pinpoint that pre-inspection grinding—a low-skill task—was consuming 16% of highly-skilled welders' time. This tangible, easily understood finding quickly gained me credibility with the team.

Outcomes & Business Impact

  • Identified $20,000/Month in Savings: By quantifying the time welders spent on grinding, I built a successful business case to create a dedicated, lower-cost grinding team, delivering an immediate ROI.
  • Pinpointed Root Causes of Defects: The analysis provided clear evidence linking high defect rates to specific joint types and inadequate training, allowing for targeted improvement actions.
  • Data-Driven HR Strategy: The findings provided HR with a data-driven basis for refining their welder selection and training programs.

Key Lessons Learned

Quantitative data is powerful, but qualitative, on-the-ground insights are essential for context and buy-in. To drive real change in an operational environment, you must build relationships and demonstrate that your analysis is rooted in the reality of the work being done.

Scrap Metal Value Recovery Initiative

Applying Lean principles to turn operational waste into a significant revenue stream.

Project Snapshot

Company: Multinational Energy Firm

My Role: Business Optimization Analyst

Tools & Frameworks

Lean (Value Stream Mapping)

ROI Analysis

Process Improvement

Business Problem

The company was selling scrap copper cable at a blended, low rate, approximately 50% below the market price for pure copper. This represented a significant and untapped revenue opportunity, as the high-value copper was not being segregated from its low-value insulation.

Challenges & My Approach

The challenge was not analytical, but operational. I had to coordinate with three different departments—Operations, Supply Chain, and HSES—each with their own priorities, to implement a new physical process. I created a simple, one-page process map and a clear RACI chart to define roles and responsibilities. By clearly articulating the financial benefit and making the new process easy to adopt, I was able to secure the cross-functional collaboration needed for a smooth implementation.

Outcomes & Business Impact

  • Immediate 665% ROI: The very first batch of segregated copper generated a net profit of $12,521 on an initial investment of just $1,882.
  • Generated $14.4K in Additional Revenue: The segregated copper from the first batch sold for a 39% higher price than it would have as mixed scrap.
  • Created a Sustainable Revenue Stream: The project established a new, ongoing process projected to generate an additional $30,000+ annually from what was previously considered waste.

Key Lessons Learned

A brilliant idea is worthless without execution. For process improvement projects, clear communication, simple documentation (like a one-page process map), and defined ownership are just as important as the financial analysis itself.

Canteen Services Cost Optimization

Using contract analysis and employee feedback to address a $2M annual expenditure.

Project Snapshot

Company: Multinational Energy Firm

My Role: Business Optimization Analyst

Tools & Frameworks

Contract Analysis

Survey Design (Microsoft Forms)

Stakeholder Management

Business Problem

The company was spending approximately $2 million annually on canteen amenities and food allowances. This significant expenditure lacked proper oversight, and the value being delivered by the third-party catering vendor was unclear.

Challenges & My Approach

The challenge was balancing cost-cutting with employee morale. I knew that any changes to food services could be met with resistance. My approach was to use a two-pronged strategy: a rigorous legal contract review to find objective cost savings, and a comprehensive employee survey to capture the subjective user experience. This allowed me to build a case that was not just about saving money, but also about responding to widespread employee dissatisfaction with hygiene and food quality.

Outcomes & Business Impact

  • Identified $83K+ in Direct Cost Savings: The contract analysis created immediate leverage to back-charge the vendor for services the company was unnecessarily paying for.
  • Built a Data-Backed Case for Re-Tendering: The survey finding that 81% of employees did not want to continue with the current caterer provided an undeniable justification for seeking a new vendor.
  • Improved Stakeholder Alignment: By combining financial data with employee feedback, I created a powerful narrative that aligned HR, Operations, and Safety on the need for change.

Key Lessons Learned

Combining quantitative data (contract analysis) with qualitative data (user feedback) creates a far more compelling argument for change than either one alone. This dual approach allows you to build a business case that satisfies both financial and human-centered objectives.

Forklift Fleet Optimization Strategy

Using data analysis to drive significant cost savings in heavy equipment management.

Project Snapshot

Company: Multinational Energy Firm

My Role: Business Optimization Analyst

Tools & Frameworks

Excel (Pareto, Cost Analysis)

Root Cause Analysis (RCA)

Asset Management

Business Problem

The company's forklift fleet of 35 units was a significant operational expense, but there was no data-driven strategy for its management. Decisions about hiring versus owning, and when to retire aging assets, were made ad-hoc. This resulted in excessive maintenance costs, poor utilization, and missed opportunities for cost savings.

My Role & Solution

I initiated and led a comprehensive analysis of the entire forklift fleet. My process was to:

  1. Analyze Fleet Data: I gathered and analyzed 31 months of data on meter readings, fuel consumption, maintenance costs, and age for all 35 forklifts.
  2. Identify Key Issues: Pareto analysis revealed that 7-ton forklifts were the most numerous but not always the most needed. A key finding was the fleet's chronic underutilization, averaging only 56% of a standard 11-hour workday. Furthermore, 70% of the fleet was over 10 years old, leading to escalating maintenance costs.
  3. Build a Business Case: I conducted a detailed cost-benefit analysis, comparing the actual hourly cost of owned equipment (including fuel, maintenance, operator) with rental market rates. This revealed that for several aging forklifts, renting was significantly cheaper.
  4. Propose Actionable Recommendations: Based on the data, I recommended scrapping the 4 most inefficient forklifts, creating a projected annual savings of over $172,000. I also designed a new process map for equipment allocation to prioritize lower-cost rentals over high-cost owned assets.

Key Findings & Business Impact

  • Identified $172K+ in Annual Savings: The data-driven recommendation to scrap 4 specific high-maintenance forklifts and optimize the hire/own strategy was projected to save over $14K per month.
  • Revealed 44% Underutilization: The analysis brought to light the fleet's poor overall efficiency, sparking a wider conversation about resource scheduling and planning.
  • Created a Data-Driven Framework: This project established a new, analytical framework for all future asset management decisions, moving the company from reactive maintenance to proactive, cost-conscious fleet strategy.

Welder Performance & Quality Improvement

A Lean Six Sigma approach to reducing production delays and improving weld quality.

Project Snapshot

Company: Multinational Energy Firm

My Role: Project Leader (LSS Green Belt)

Tools & Frameworks

Lean Six Sigma (DMAIC)

Root Cause Analysis (RCA)

Pareto Analysis, Fishbone Diagram

Statistical Analysis (Chi-Square)

Business Problem

A critical energy project was plagued by significant production delays caused by a bottleneck in the welding inspection process. The time from weld completion to the final Non-Destructive Testing (NDT) was unacceptably long, and a high NDT rejection rate of 18.15% was forcing costly and time-consuming rework.

My Role & Solution

As the project leader, I applied the DMAIC (Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control) methodology. In the "Analyze" phase, I conducted a deep-dive RCA:

  1. Process Mapping & Data Analysis: I mapped the end-to-end process and analyzed delay data, finding that 35% of delays exceeded 10 days. A key insight was that pre-inspection grinding, a low-skill task, was being performed by highly-skilled, expensive welders, consuming 16% of their total time.
  2. Pareto & Cause-and-Effect Analysis: A Pareto analysis of visual inspection rejections showed that "Toe Grinding" issues were a primary cause (55%). A fishbone diagram helped identify welder training and technique as key areas for improvement.
  3. Statistical Analysis: I analyzed welder performance data, discovering that 72% of "Below Average" welders were from subcontracted labor. A Chi-Square test confirmed a statistically significant relationship between welder experience and performance.

Key Findings & Business Impact

  • Identified $20,000/Month in Savings: By quantifying the time welders spent on grinding, I built a successful business case to create a dedicated, lower-cost grinding team. This freed up skilled welders to focus on high-value work, delivering an immediate ROI.
  • Pinpointed Root Causes of Defects: The analysis provided clear evidence linking high defect rates to specific joint types, inadequate training, and underperforming subcontractors, allowing for targeted improvement actions.
  • Data-Driven HR Strategy: The findings provided HR and procurement with a data-driven basis for blacklisting underperforming labor suppliers and refining their welder selection and training programs.

Scrap Metal Value Recovery Initiative

Applying Lean principles to turn operational waste into a significant revenue stream.

Project Snapshot

Company: Multinational Energy Firm

My Role: Business Optimization Analyst

Tools & Frameworks

Lean Six Sigma (Value Stream)

ROI Analysis

Process Improvement

Business Problem

The company was selling scrap copper cable wire at a blended, low rate of $3,833/ton, approximately 50% below the London Metal Exchange (LME) price for pure copper. This represented a significant and untapped revenue opportunity, as the high-value copper was not being segregated from its low-value insulation.

My Role & Solution

I identified this value leakage and led a cross-functional team (Operations, Supply Chain, HSES) to implement a simple, high-impact solution. My approach was:

  1. Quantify the Opportunity: I analyzed a sample batch, determining that the cable had a copper-to-insulation ratio of approximately 70:30. This data proved that the potential value of the separated copper far exceeded the current scrap price.
  2. Propose a Low-Cost Solution: I researched and proposed the purchase of a wire stripping machine and surveillance cameras for secure storage, with a total one-time investment of only $1,882.
  3. Execute and Measure: I managed the implementation of the new process. We stripped the copper from the first batch of scrap cable and sold it as a separate commodity.

Key Findings & Business Impact

  • Immediate 665% ROI: The very first batch of segregated copper generated a net profit of $12,521 on an initial investment of just $1,882.
  • Generated $14.4K in Additional Revenue: The segregated copper from the first batch sold for $51,351, compared to the $36,949 it would have fetched as mixed cable scrap—a 39% uplift.
  • Created a New, Sustainable Revenue Stream: The project established a new, ongoing process projected to generate an additional $30,000+ annually from what was previously considered low-value waste.

The company's Oracle Inventory system lacked a coherent structure. With duplicated locators and no defined hierarchy, it was impossible to get an accurate picture of warehouse space or capacity. This led to inefficient use of space, difficulty in locating materials, and an inability to perform any capacity planning.

Analytical Approach

My approach was strategic, focusing on redesigning the foundational data architecture of the inventory system. The analysis involved auditing the existing disorganized structure, identifying all redundancies and logical inconsistencies. The solution was to create a new, standardized data model to serve as a robust foundation for all warehouse operations and future analytics.

Process & Key Findings

The analysis revealed over 20,000 locator entries, with thousands of duplicates. The key finding was that the lack of structure was the root cause of widespread inefficiency. I designed a new hierarchical model that separated physical and logical sub-inventories and introduced new data fields for locator type (e.g., Rack, Floor), area type (e.g., Indoor AC), and, crucially, physical capacity (dimensions and safe working load).

Strategic Insights & Deliverables

This project underscored the principle of "garbage in, garbage out." A clean, logical data structure is a prerequisite for efficient operations. The new system enabled, for the first time, true capacity management and data-driven material slotting. My deliverable was the functional requirements document that defined this new data model, providing the blueprint to transform the chaotic inventory system into an organized, analyzable asset.

End-to-End Material Traceability Reporting

Consolidating disparate Oracle data to create a unified view of the material lifecycle.

Project Snapshot

Project Type: Descriptive Analytics

Company: Industrial Manufacturing Firm

My Role: Business Analyst

Tools & Frameworks

Business Intelligence (BI)

Report Design

Oracle ERP

Business Problem

To understand the full history of a single piece of material, a manager had to manually pull and cross-reference data from multiple, disconnected Oracle reports (MR, PO, GRN, MIN, etc.). This time-consuming process hindered quick decision-making and made it difficult to provide clients with a simple, consolidated status update for their materials.

Analytical Approach

This was a descriptive analytics project aimed at data consolidation and visualization. The approach was to design a single, unified report that could programmatically join data from different Oracle modules based on a common identifier (e.g., Part ID, LEL ID). The goal was to present a chronological, easy-to-read history of every transaction related to an item.

Process & Key Findings

I analyzed the data schemas of the various Oracle modules to map the relationships between them. The key finding was that all the necessary data existed but was siloed. I designed a "Complete Traceability Report" that structured this data logically, from initial receipt through all transfers, returns, and final issuance. I also designed a simplified, client-facing "Material Register" for live status updates.

Strategic Insights & Deliverables

The project's insight is that the value of data is multiplied when it is connected. By breaking down data silos, we could provide a holistic view that was previously impossible to achieve efficiently. My deliverable was the functional requirements document for these new reports, which provided a single source of truth for the entire material lifecycle and significantly accelerated the time-to-insight for managers.

QR Code-Based Digital Warehouse System

Digitizing warehouse operations with a scannable labeling system and mobile application.

Project Snapshot

Project Type: Strategic & Prescriptive Analytics

Company: Industrial Manufacturing Firm

My Role: Business Analyst

Tools & Frameworks

Digital Transformation

Mobile Application Design

QR Code Technology

Business Problem

Accessing inventory information on the warehouse floor was a major operational bottleneck. Staff had to leave their work area to use a desktop computer or rely on cumbersome printouts to get details about an item. This lack of immediate, point-of-use data slowed down critical tasks like cycle counting, auditing, and material picking.

Analytical Approach

The approach was to prescribe a digital transformation solution for a key area (the pipe shop) to serve as a proof-of-concept. The analysis involved identifying the most critical data points needed by floor staff and designing the most efficient delivery mechanism. A QR code system paired with a mobile app was identified as the optimal, low-cost solution.

Process & Key Findings

I designed a multi-level QR labeling system: one for individual items (encoding Part ID), one for racks (encoding location), and one for parts bins (encoding Part ID). The key finding was that this structure allowed for maximum flexibility. Scanning a rack would show all contents, while scanning a bin would show its stock levels across the entire company. I designed the screen flows and UI for the companion mobile app to display this live Oracle data.

Strategic Insights & Deliverables

This project demonstrated that empowering frontline workers with real-time data is a powerful driver of efficiency. The QR code system acts as a simple, effective bridge between physical assets and their rich digital records in the ERP. My deliverable was the functional requirements document defining the entire system, from label design to the mobile app's UI/UX, creating a roadmap to digitize on-the-floor operations and enhance traceability.

Automated Shelf-Life & Expiry Tracking System

Integrating proactive expiry monitoring into Oracle ERP to prevent waste and ensure compliance.

Project Snapshot

Project Type: Predictive & Prescriptive Analytics

Company: Industrial Manufacturing Firm

My Role: Business Analyst

Tools & Frameworks

Oracle ERP Customization

Process Automation

Risk Mitigation

Business Problem

The company was manually tracking the shelf life of perishable consumables (paints, chemicals) in a physical logbook. This reactive process had no predictive capabilities, leading to a high risk of material expiring on the shelf, resulting in financial loss and non-compliance with quality procedures that required 6-monthly deterioration checks.

Analytical Approach

The approach was to build a predictive and prescriptive system within Oracle. The system would use the expiry date data to predict which items were at risk and prescribe actions via automated alerts. The analysis involved defining the business rules for these alerts (e.g., "notify 30 days before expiry") and designing the system enhancements to support them.

Process & Key Findings

The process began with designing new "Expiry Date" fields within Oracle's item creation and receiving screens to capture the data at its source. The key finding was that by making this a mandatory field for certain item categories, we could guarantee the data's availability. I then specified the logic for an automated reporting system and, most importantly, for an automated email notification system to proactively flag items nearing expiry.

Strategic Insights & Deliverables

This project showed that automation is a powerful tool for risk mitigation. By transforming a manual, reactive task into an automated, proactive system, we could prevent financial losses and ensure compliance with quality standards. My deliverable was the functional requirements document detailing the necessary Oracle screen modifications and the logic for the automated alerting system, effectively creating an early-warning system for inventory waste.
